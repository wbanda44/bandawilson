Share Market

Check out the key stock trading terms for beginners.

Investments Field Analysis

The main objective of investment analysis is to help individuals, entrepreneurs and practitioners understand the investment field. Below, key concepts are presented to provide appreciation of the practice of investments through simple definitions of key terms used in the investment field.

BROKER

A person who buys or sells an investment for you, in exchange for a fee

BUY

Means to take a position or to buy shares in a company.

SELL

Getting rid of the shares you purchased, either because you have achieved your goals or just want to cut your loses.

BID

Your bid is the amount of money you are willing to pay for a share.

ASK

ASK is the amount of money the people selling the share are looking to get for their shares.

BID-ASK SPREAD

The bid-ask spread is the difference between what you want to invest and what the broke wants to get. the spread must be resolved before the transaction can take place.

BULL MARKET

A bull market is a market condition that means stock prices are expected to rise.

BEAR MARKET

A bear market is one in which investors expect share prices to fall.

LIMIT ORDER

A limit order provides instruction to only execute at or under a purchase price or at or above a sale price.

MARKET ORDER

A market order provides instructions to execute a transaction as quickly as possible at the current market price.

GOOD TILL CANCELLED ORDER

A GTC order means that your order stands untill you cancel it and will be executed whenever the stock price comes to your order price.

DAY ORDER

Day order means that your order is good only for the day it was placed.

VOLATILITY

Volatility simply means how fast the stock price is moving up or down.

LIQUIDITY

Liquidity refers to how easily you can get into and out of a stock.

TRADING VOLUME

Trading volume is the number of shares being traded each day, a factor that has huge implications for a stocks liquidity.

GOING LONG

You're expecting that the company's stock will increase in price so that you can buy low and sell high.

AVERAGING DOWN

This is when an investor buys more of a stock as the price goes down. This results in a decrease of the average price at which the investor purchased the stock.

DAY TRADING

The practice of buying and selling within the same trading day, before the close of the markets on that day.

DIVIDEND

This is the portion of the company's earnings that is paid to the shareholders, the people that own that company's stock, on a quarterly or annual basis.

EXCHANGE

An exchange is a place in which different investments are traded. The most well-known, New York Stock Exchange, NASDAQ, etc.

EXECUTION

When an order to buy or sell shares has been completed.

MARGIN

A margin account lets you borrow money from a broker to purchase shares. The different between the amount of the loan and the price of the securities is called the margin.

MOVING AVERAGE

A stock's average price-per-share during a specific periodof time.

PORTFOLIO

A collection of investments owned by an investor.

QUOTE

Information on a stock's latest trading price. This is sometimes delayed by 20 minutes.

RALLY

A rapid increase in the general price level of the market or of the price of an individual stock.

SECTOR

A group of stocks that are in the same business. An example would be the "Technology" sector, including companies like Apple and Microsoft.

ETFs

ETFs are exchange traded funds. They're like stocks, because you buy and sell shares, but they're also like mutual funds, because they track an index.

ADRs

ADRs are American Depository Reciepts for foreign companies that are traded in the US.

BETA

A measurement of the relationship between the price of stock and the movement of the whole market. e.g. If stock X has a beta of 1.5, that means that for every 1 point move in the market, stock X moves 1.5 points and vice versa.

CAPITALIZATION

Market capitalization refers to what the market thinks a company's value is.

BLUE CHIP STOCKS

These are the large, industry-leading companies offering stable dividend payments.

SECONDARY OFFERING

If a company's stock is closing well, they may do another offering in order to sell more stock and raise more money.

PUBLIC FLOAT

This is the number of shares that can actually trade, once shares that insiders (like the company's C-suite and early investors) control are subtracted.

AUTHORIZED SHARES

This is the total number of shares that a company can trade. It's always bigger than the public float.

IPO

An IPO is an initial price offering, which happens when a private company becomes a publicly traded company, in order to raise money.

FOREX

FOREX, Foreign Exchange involves trading different currencies.

HEDGE/MUTUAL FUNDS

Hedge funds and Mutual funds are two different types of investment accounts that you can buy into. They turn around and invest your money in dozens, hundreds or even thousands os stock.
